Inspiration

December 23, 2020

In October 2018 I spent a brief holiday in Germany. The first day I landed in Berlin and immediately made my way to Mauer Park for the weekly Sunday gathering inlcuding street food, karaoke, and a flea market. After sitting down with a gyro and a beer to relax, I began to take in the unique mix of people, sights, and sounds.

‘Berlin, the greatest cultural extravaganza that one could imagine.’ -David Bowie
Through the field of people gathering was a man with a small plastic pool (the one that can fit a few toddlers at best). Which I thought was strange to have at a park that time of year. With kids gathered around, all the sudden he lifted long wands from the pool and a slew of bubbles flew off the wands and into the air.


I watched for a while as I took in the rest of the scenery. It was a magical day. And out of that trip came the idea to make bubbles as a birthday gift for my newphew. While the surprise gift of bubbling went extremely well (granted it was his 1st birthday), what I didn't expect was for me to continue bubbling as a hobby. It is now part of tradition to bring bubbles when I visit my family, for friends' birthday parties, and pretty much every park gathering. While I enjoy bubbling for myself, my family, and my friends, it is most enjoyable to see the magic through the lense of a child enjoying the bubbles.
